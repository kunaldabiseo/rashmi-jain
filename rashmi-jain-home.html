The practitioner

Rashmi Jain.
The practice behind the practice.

For more than fifteen years, Rashmi has held space for the people who arrive at yoga not for fitness, but for something quieter — a way to live inside their own bodies again. Her training crosses the traditional Hatha lineage, modern yoga therapy, and clinical mental-health counselling.

Her work moves between one-to-one trauma-informed therapy, small-group practice in Jaipur, seasonal retreats in the Himalayas, and certification programmes that have trained teachers across India, Singapore and the UK.

"We don't practice to escape the noise. We practice to stop calling our own minds noise."

She has been featured by Vogue India, The Wellness Issue, Outlook Mind+Body and across leading publications on contemplative practice and mental health.

How the work moves

A four-step arc.
No theatrics, no magic.

Every engagement — personal, corporate or educational — moves through the same disciplined arc. Specific to you, predictable to me. We measure the change in your sleep, breath and rhythm — not in the language of healing.
Step 0120 min · Free

Listening Call

A short conversation to understand what you are carrying and whether this practice is the right fit. No assessment, no obligation, no fee.

Step 0275 min · Paid

Assessment

A structured 75-minute intake — breath, body, sleep, history. At the end you'll have a written practice plan, not a vague intention to "feel better."

Step 038 – 12 sessions

Practice Engagement

A time-bound engagement with measurable markers — typically eight to twelve sessions, weekly or fortnightly, depending on what we're holding together.

Step 043-month aftercare

Integration

Three months of light-touch follow-through — monthly check-ins, refinements to your home practice, and a clean way out when the work has held.

Honest answers

Things you might be
wondering.

If your question isn't here, write to hello@rashmijain.com — Rashmi or someone from her practice will reply within one working day.
Is this yoga, or is this therapy?
It is both, held in the same room. Yoga therapy uses breath, movement and stillness as clinical tools — not as fitness. The counselling is conventional talk-based practice, paced and contained. Most clients arrive needing both, and we shape the proportions to you.
Do I need to be flexible? Or already practising yoga?
No — most people who come to this practice have never been on a yoga mat, and many arrive specifically because conventional yoga felt too performative. The work begins with breath, slow movement and listening. Flexibility is never the goal; presence is.
What does an engagement typically cost?
It depends on scope. A single intake session is priced very differently from a 12-session engagement, a corporate retainer or a teacher training. Specific numbers are shared after the free listening call — once we know what we're holding together.
Do you offer sessions online?
Yes — most one-to-one work runs over secure video, and a meaningful portion of the practice happens in your own home anyway. In-person sessions are available in Jaipur by appointment, and retreats are held seasonally in the Himalayas and Pondicherry.
I'm training my organisation's wellness lead — how does that work?
Two flagship cohorts run twice a year — the 200-hour foundational training and the advanced trauma-informed certificate. Companies, clinics and schools typically enrol two to four members per cycle. A custom in-house version can also be built for your team.
How quickly will you respond to my form?
Within one working day — usually the same evening. Rashmi or her practice manager reads every form personally. There is no call-centre, no automated funnel, and no follow-up beyond the one reply unless you ask for it.
